# Cardio-Kidney-Metabolic (CKM) Health

## How the heart, kidneys, and metabolism are connected

Cardio-kidney-metabolic health, sometimes called CKM, describes the close
connection between the heart and blood vessels, the kidneys, and the body's
metabolism (including blood sugar, body weight, and cholesterol). These systems
talk to each other constantly. When one is under strain, the others are often
affected too.

For example, high blood pressure and diabetes can damage the kidneys over time.
Reduced kidney function can then make the heart work harder and make it easier
for fluid to build up. Excess weight and insulin resistance add further strain
to both the heart and the kidneys. This is why doctors increasingly look at the
whole picture rather than treating each organ in isolation.

## Why this matters for HFpEF

Heart failure with preserved ejection fraction sits right in the middle of this
web. Many people with HFpEF also have chronic kidney disease, type 2 diabetes,
obesity, or high blood pressure. Treating the metabolic and kidney pieces is
often part of treating the heart failure itself.

## Shared risk factors

The main shared risk factors across cardio-kidney-metabolic conditions include
high blood pressure, high blood sugar or diabetes, excess body weight,
unhealthy cholesterol levels, smoking, and physical inactivity. Because these
factors overlap, improving one often helps several conditions at once.

## Medicines that help more than one system

Some medicines are valued precisely because they protect more than one organ
system. SGLT2 inhibitors, for instance, can help the heart, support kidney
function, and help with blood sugar. This overlap is part of why they have
become important in cardio-kidney-metabolic care.

## What people can do

Common recommendations include keeping blood pressure and blood sugar in target
ranges, supporting a healthy weight, staying active within safe limits, not
smoking, and having kidney function checked regularly through blood and urine
tests. A care team can help set realistic, personalised targets.
